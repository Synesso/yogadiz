About

My interest in yoga probably began watching the exotic Swami Saraswati on TV in the late 1960s. Then as a college student I came across "Autobiography of a Yogi", the inspired and incredible life story of Yogananda, the yogi who helped to introduce yoga to the west.

After college I travelled through Asia, spending 6 months in India. It was very intoxicating and definitely gave me a different perspective on life.

Back home I found myself spending a long weekend at Satyananda Ashram in northern NSW. A friend had taken me there. It fascinated me and motivated me to come home and find a teacher.

I attended classes with several Satyananda teachers and loved them. But the teachers moved on leaving me still looking for that teacher to commit to.

I eventually found Glenda Gooding in 1988 and began an earnest and passionate journey as one of her many dedicated students.

Glenda ran the Sherbrooke Centre in Belgrave Heights in the Dandenong Ranges. I made the trip up and down those hills from the eastern suburbs of Melbourne for many years.

Glenda encouraged me to do my teacher training and I completed her very satisfying and life-changing course in 1993.

I know now that finding the right teacher was pivotal in my journey of becoming a teacher.

With qualifications in hand the practical process of becoming a good teacher begins. Finding students and consolidating what you have learnt and bringing it into the classroom is a slow, evolving process.

I worked from my home, in local leisure centres and community houses, health centres, schools and businesses. I accepted all opportunities that came my way. I continued as I do today to attend classes, workshops and retreats and remain interested in ongoing learning. I have a continuous appetite for reading as much yoga literature that I can find.

As a teacher of many years I feel I am now able to teach from my own truth and experience and feel my teaching has become more intuitive and inspired. I have maintained my "tapas", deep passion towards teaching and practice and have remained continuously curious and enthusiastic.

For this I would like to thank all the many and varied teachers who have inspired me on this ongoing journey.

Style

A hatha yoga approach which uses mindful movement and posture, breath awareness, relaxation, meditation and yoga philosophy to help guide students into a deeper understanding of self.

These classes pay homage to the traditional model of yoga.

Classes are considerate and sensitive to the individual needs of the students. Classes allow time for people to connect and reflect.

The intention is to help students bring the wisdom of yoga into their practice and their daily lives.
